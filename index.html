WE ARE NOT SUSTAINABLE

YET...

Here is where we're at

Nobody knows what sustainable fashion might look like

but... we want to be the first to find out

We’ll continue to deliver innovative, concious and authentic fashion for you, at a price you can afford while constantly working in the background to always and forever decrease our carbon footprint, our water usage, increase our renewable energy percentage and investigate our supply chain deeper and deeper to provide more than just a fair working wage for our workers, but having a positive impact on their life in our own beautiful way.

and... Silfen believes in actions over words

Above is our interactive roadmap of sustainabilty goals. There we’ll share with you our big wins, our setbacks, our needs, our small steps and our big steps. As a tiny company, right now consisting of 8 employees, a lot of goals lay further down the line, where growth will allow us bigger seats at the table and we can change the world for the better.

Steps we’ve taken so far

Reducing water consumption

We try to minimize the water consumption in our production in an effort to decrease its negative impact on the environment and to ensure local access to clean water.

Currently we use 50% organic cotton from our which uses 91% less water than conventionally grown cotton. This significantly increase the cost of manufacturer but we it’s worth paying for.

With economies of scale there is a chance in the future we can negotiate a more viable price for this material. Then we can increase the percentage we use without dramatically passing on the cost to you guys.

shipping

Shipping is a huge contributor to global emission and pollution. In the future we plan to bring production closer to home in EU which will dramatically reduce our footprint.

Until then we plan to ship entirely by land as opposed to air or sea which is much cleaner and more efficient.

However due to very sad political situations in both Afghanistan and Ukraine this is next to impossible at the moment.

Returning to land shipping is a top priority for us.

Working conditions

Aspiring to sustainability goes beyond materials. Our supply chain involves many people in countries far away, and we must work to make sure than their standard of living and opportunities are fair.

All the workers at all of our factories have pensions to guarantee them financial stability in later life.

We also provide insurance for all workers and their families.

We visit our manufacturers regularly to ensure they follow the principles regarding working conditions, anti discrimination policies and quality control etc.

All our manufacturers are sa8000 certified.

Despite all this the regulations will be easier to enforcer and improve upon when this happens in Europe, so we’re also endevouring to move production here as soon as possible.

Materials

Cotton

In addition to being one of the most widely grown crops in the world, growing conventional cotton is also one of the most chemical-­intensive. These chemicals have tremendous impact on the earth’s air, water, soil, and the health of people in cotton-growing areas. They are among the most toxic chemicals as classified by the Environmental Protection Agency.

Leather

Leather will never be an animal-friendly product: It is made of dead animal skin. However, the skin used to make leather comes from animals raised for their meat. In that sense, it uses a byproduct from another industry, so it doesn't actually need additional land and resources.

Vegan

Most people are unaware of how many animal products are used in clothing. From Animal based glue, feathers, wool, silk, and various lacquers.

Rpet Line

RPET is the short name often used for recycled polyethylene tetraphyte (PET). It is the world’s fourth most common plastic resin, used in everything from food packaging to clothing. When you see the word ‘RPET’ it simply means that the PET in the product you’re looking at, should come from a recycled, pre-existing source.
